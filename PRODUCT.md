# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Design recruiters, hiring managers, and design directors at London-based and UK/Taiwan tech companies. They are fluent in design quality: they can read a portfolio in minutes and calibrate seniority, process rigour, and visual taste simultaneously. They visit on desktop during office hours, often comparing multiple candidates. They are pattern-matching for "does this person actually think like a designer, or just produce deliverables?"

## Product Purpose

A job-hunting portfolio for Rowan Lin, a UI/UX Designer specialising in cross-screen interfaces (mobile, TV, multi-platform systems). The portfolio must communicate: research-led process, visual maturity, and cross-device system thinking. Success = a recruiter books a call within the session.

## Brand Personality

Clean · Methodical · Trustworthy. Rowan is the designer who has done the research before anyone else in the room. The portfolio should feel considered, not flashy. Quiet confidence, not performance.

## Anti-references

- Generic Webflow portfolio template (same hero layout, same card grid, same "Let's work together" footer)
- Corporate/formal: stiff, lifeless, reads like a company site not a person's work
- Loud motion portfolios: every element animated, feels like showing off craft over communication

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't describe** — let the work carry the argument; copy supports, never substitutes
2. **Process as evidence** — research and iteration are the differentiator, surface them early
3. **Earned restraint** — minimal not because it's safe, but because every element has a job
4. **Cross-screen credibility** — the portfolio itself must work flawlessly at every size, or it undermines the designer's stated expertise
5. **Recruiter-paced** — optimised for a 90-second skim that earns a 10-minute read

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Custom cursor (non-standard) must degrade gracefully on touch devices. Motion should respect prefers-reduced-motion.
