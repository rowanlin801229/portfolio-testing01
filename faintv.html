01 — Streaming Platform · Multi-device · 1 Year

FainTV

Role UI/UX Designer
Duration 1 Year
Platform TV · Mobile · Web
Company Hawsing Co., Ltd
Streaming Provider, Taiwan
Overview

FainTV is a prominent streaming service provider in Taiwan, offering an extensive library of live television, movies, and hit dramas. As the UI/UX Designer, I spearheaded the complete redesign of the FainTV 2.0 platform — modernising the viewing journey and creating a seamless, intuitive interface that enhances how audiences discover and interact with digital content across multiple devices.

Problem

Traditional TV interfaces rely on remote control text input, creating significant barriers for elderly users.

Research Finding

65% of elderly users struggled with text-based searching. The remote-control keyboard created friction during content search and account registration, causing high drop-off rates and low task completion — especially for a demographic that represents a significant portion of the FainTV user base.

30% Before redesign
80% After redesign

Task completion rate among elderly users increased by 50 percentage points — significantly lowering the barrier to accessing digital services.

Process

Double Diamond — every decision rooted in data, not assumptions.

Framework

I utilised the Double Diamond framework to ensure every design decision began with extensive primary and secondary research to uncover core user pain points. By synthesising insights into personas and empathy maps, I bridged the gap between user needs and business goals — delivering a scalable design system that simplifies complexity and enhances the entertainment experience.

01
Define Objective

Identifying core business goals and key questions to answer through user insights.

02
Plan Research

Selecting qualitative and quantitative methods to gather comprehensive data.

03
Recruit

Screening 50+ participants for a diverse, representative sample.

04
Conduct Research

In-depth interviews capturing real-time behaviours and frustrations.

05
Analyse Data

Synthesising findings into personas and user flows.

Solution

Voice input and landline login — directly addressing the two core pain points.

Design Decision

I introduced voice input to replace remote control typing, and landline phone number login to replace email and mobile verification — directly addressing the two core pain points identified through user interviews and usability testing. Both solutions required zero learning curve for elderly users.

01
Intelligent Voice Search

Streamlines navigation by allowing users to locate content via a 3-second remote button press, reducing a complex search to just two steps.

02
Immersive T-Commerce

Enables real-time shopping during broadcasts, allowing viewers to purchase featured items without disrupting the viewing experience.

03
Structured Account Management

Utilises a 12-column grid system to organise diverse member data, ensuring a balanced and responsive layout across all platforms.

04
Optimised Checkout Flow

Simplifies the multi-step purchasing journey into a linear path optimised for D-pad remote navigation and high conversion.

Reflection & Growth

I mastered the nuances of 10-foot UI design while navigating complex cross-platform integrations, significantly strengthening my ability to lead cross-functional collaborations and resolve technical conflicts.

Scaling Impact & Safety

Moving forward, I aim to transform the TV remote into a safety tool for the elderly by integrating emergency SOS features — creating a more inclusive and secure product ecosystem for every home.
